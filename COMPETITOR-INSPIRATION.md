## Design Patterns Summary

### Common Patterns Across All 12 Sites

- **Color trends:** Dark hero sections are near-universal — 10 out of 12 sites use dark overlays on hero photos. Accent colour families split into: warm tones (orange, amber/gold, coral — 5 sites), blue (3 sites), green/teal (2 sites), and muted earth tones (2 sites). White text on dark backgrounds is the standard hero treatment. The SA market strongly favours warm accents (orange, gold), while international sites show more variety.

- **Typography:** Sans-serif dominates — Open Sans is the most common body font (4/12 sites), followed by Poppins, Raleway, and system fonts. Heading fonts are more varied, with bold uppercase sans-serif being the most common hero treatment. The most distinctive sites (Pinnacle, Thomas DC) use serif or display fonts for headings to convey premium quality. Abel, Raleway, and Poppins appear as heading fonts. Only 1 site (Thomas DC) uses serif body text.

- **Hero style:** Full-width photo backgrounds with dark overlays are the dominant pattern (10/12). Construction imagery is universal — workers, buildings, blueprints, architectural renders, and completed projects. 2 sites break the pattern: Devco with a split-screen branded layout, and JBS with a minimalist dark-mode approach. Most heroes feature a single prominent CTA button. Only 2/12 include lead capture forms directly in the hero.

- **Trust signals:** Years in business is the most common trust element (mentioned by 5/12). NHBRC certification appears on SA sites. Project portfolios and galleries are the primary proof-of-work mechanism. Google review scores are notably absent from most homepages — a significant gap. Professional affiliations and certifications get dedicated pages on the more established sites. WhatsApp floating buttons appear on 3/12 SA sites.

- **CTA patterns:** "Get a Free Quote" / "Request a Free Estimate" / "Enquire" is the dominant CTA text across all markets. Phone numbers are visible in headers on most SA sites. Luxury/premium sites (Thomas DC, Devco) deliberately omit prominent CTAs, relying on portfolio quality instead. WhatsApp integration appears exclusively on SA sites.

- **Layout flow:** Hero (photo + headline + CTA) -> Services overview -> About/trust section -> Projects/portfolio -> Contact/footer. Most sites follow a linear scroll pattern. International sites tend to have cleaner layouts with more white space, while KZN sites pack more content above the fold.

- **Quality gap:** There is a noticeable quality gap between the international sites and most KZN local competitors. Perth and Dallas sites feature higher-quality photography, more refined typography, and more cohesive brand identities. Cape Town sites (JBS, Grow) bridge this gap. Most KZN sites use template-style designs with generic photography.

### Differentiation Opportunities for Plan Elite KZN

1. **Modern visual identity with colour contrast** — Most KZN competitors use generic dark heroes with standard fonts. Plan Elite could stand out with a bold, contemporary colour palette (e.g., deep navy with a vibrant accent like electric orange or lime green) and a distinctive display font. Pinnacle Construction is the only local competitor with a premium feel — there is room for a second strong brand in this space. The Devco Builders creative layout shows how breaking conventions can create memorability.

2. **Portfolio-first approach with project galleries** — Most KZN competitor sites are text-heavy and lead-gen focused with minimal visual proof of work. A site that leads with striking project photography (before/after shots, aerial drone views of developments) would immediately differentiate. The Thomas DC approach of letting beautiful photography sell the work is the gold standard. Interactive project galleries or case studies would stand out significantly in the Durban market.

3. **Comprehensive service breadth as a selling point** — Plan Elite covers building construction, roofing, painting, tiling, nutec houses, paving, landscaping, awnings, and cladding. Most competitors focus on 2-3 services. A well-designed services hub that showcases this breadth with clear visual categories would position Plan Elite as a one-stop-shop — a strong differentiator in the KZN market where most contractors specialise narrowly.

4. **Google reviews and social proof prominence** — None of the 12 competitor sites prominently feature Google review scores in their hero or above-the-fold content. Featuring a strong review score (e.g., "4.8 stars from 50+ reviews") prominently would be a powerful trust signal that competitors are missing entirely.

5. **Aspirational lifestyle imagery** — Grow Construction (Cape Town) and Thomas DC (Dallas) show the power of aspirational imagery. Instead of generic construction photos, showcasing beautiful completed homes in a KZN context (subtropical gardens, ocean views, modern interior design) would elevate Plan Elite above the utilitarian look of most local competitors.
